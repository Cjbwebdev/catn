Common car buying questions

Used car prices don't tend to fluctuate depending on the time of year. However, there are periods on the calendar where there may be more choice on the market, or you may be in a stronger negotiating position.

The busiest months for new car sales are March and September, which results in lots of part exchanges becoming available on the used market, giving you more choice. You may be able to negotiate a better price in April and October, when dealers are looking to move on cars that haven't sold.

In the UK, it's considered normal for a car to have covered anywhere, between 10,000-15,000 miles per year. So a 3 year old car with 30,000 miles would have average mileage.

More often than not, the older a car is, the more miles it will have on it. Higher mileage cars will often need a bit more maintenance than lower mileage examples, meaning you might need to replace things like brake parts and batteries. A detailed service history and maintenance file will help you understand what's already been done, and what might be needed in the future to keep the car in good working order.

All listings on MOTORS have a clear indicator in the "running costs" tab to let you know if the car you're viewing is ULEZ compliant. To search for ULEZ-compliant cars only, toggle the check box to show ULEZ compliant vehicles only.

Every car on MOTORS goes through an HPI check before to being advertised on our site, with the results clearly displayed on the individual vehicle listings. This check covers things like whether or not the car is stolen, scrapped, imported, exported, an insurance write-off or even had a colour change in its lifetime.

Please note our vehicle history check is for guidance only and all details should be confirmed with the seller before purchasing.

The vast majority of sellers are happy to let you to test drive any of the used cars they have for sale before you buy. Test drives can be easily arranged directly with the seller.
